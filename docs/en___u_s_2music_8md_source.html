3 ## Overview
4 
5 Music is a local music playing application with brand-new UI design and state-of-the-art playing experience featured with local music scanning and lyric sync functions, etc.
6 
7 ![main](fig/main1.png)
8 
9 ## Guide
10 
11 You can run, close, and create a shortcut for Music in the following ways.
12 
13 ### Run Music
14 
15 1. Click ![launcher_icon](../common/deepin_launcher.svg) in the dock to enter the Launcher interface.
16 2. Locate ![music_icon_24](../common/music_icon24.svg) by scrolling mouse wheel or searching "music" in the Launcher interface.
17 3. Right-click the Music application icon and you can:
18  - Click **Send to desktop** to create a desktop shortcut.
19  - Click **Send to dock** to fix the application on dock.
20  - Click **Add to startup** to add the application to startup and it will automatically run when the system starts up.
21 
22 > ![notes](../common/notes.svg) Notes: If Music has been fixed in the Dock, you can also click ![music_icon24](../common/music_icon24.svg) in the Dock to run it.
23 
24 ### Minimize to System Tray
25 
26 Music can be minimized to the system tray.
27 
28 - Click ![icon_menu](../common/icon_menu.svg) > **Settings** on the music interface and check **Minimize to system tray**. Music will be minimized directly to the system tray each time you close the main window.
29 - Click ![close_icon](../common/close.svg) on the Music interface to close the main window, select **Minimize to system tray** and click **Confirm**.
30 
31 > ![notes](../common/notes.svg) Notes: Check **Do not ask again** and the pop-up window will no longer be displayed.
32 
33 Right-click the Music application icon in the tray and you can perform the following operations:
34 
35  - Click **Play/Pause** to play/pause the song.
36  - Click **Previous** to play the previous song.
37  - Click **Next** to play the next song.
38  - Click **Exit** to exit Music.
39 
40 ### Exit Music
41 
42 - On the Music interface, click ![close_icon](../common/close.svg) and select **Exit** to exit Music.
43 - Right-click the Music application icon ![music_icon_24](../common/music_icon24.svg) in the Dock and select **Close All** to exit Music.
44 - Right-click ![music_icon-24](../common/music_icon24.svg) in the system tray and select **Exit** to exit Music.
45 
46 ### View Shortcuts
47 
48 On the Music interface, press **Ctrl** + **Shift** + **?** to view the shortcuts. Proficiency in shortcuts will greatly improve your efficiency.
49

121 ## Common Operations
122 
123 You can add, delete, and view info of the local music in Music in the following ways.
124 
125 ### Add Music
126 
127 On the Music interface, you can:
128 - Click **Add Music**, and add local music to the playlist, or click **Scan** to add music if there is no music file in Music.
129 - Click **Open Folder** to customize the local song directory, and add music files in batch if there is no music file in Music.
130 - Click ![icon_menu](../common/icon_menu.svg)> **Add music** to add music files to the playlist.
131 - Drag the music file/folder to the Music interface, and add the music file to the playlist.
132 
133 > ![notes](../common/notes.svg) Notes: If you are using a computer equipped with touch-screen or touchpad, you can use touchscreen gestures to operate instead of the mouse: Hold down the music file with one finger and drag it to the Music interface to add music.
134 
135 ### Search Music
136 
137 1. In the search box on top of the Music interface, you can click ![search](../common/search.svg) to enter keywords.
138 
139 2. Press the **Enter** key on the keyboard to conduct search.
140 
141  - When there is matched information, click **Play All** in the search result list to play the music in the search result list.
142  - When there is no matched information, **No search results** will be displayed in the search result list.
143 
144 3. Click ![0|close](../common/close.svg) or delete the entered information in the search box to clear the current information or cancel the search.
145 
146 ### Play Music
147 
148 You can play music in the following ways:
149 
150 - Select a music in the playlist, and double-click or right-click it to select **Play** to play the music.
151 - Select a list under the playlist in the left navigation bar. Right-click and select **Play** to play all the songs in the list according to the current order.
152 - Click **Play All** to play all the songs in the current list according to the current order.
153 
154 > ![tips](../common/tips.svg) Tips: On the Music interface, click ![play_normal](../common/play_normal.svg) to play the selected music. Click ![suspend_normal](../common/suspend_normal.svg) to pause the play. Click ![last_normal](../common/last_normal.svg) or ![next_normal](../common/next_normal.svg) to switch to the previous or the next song according to the current play mode.
155 
156 ### Add Music to My Favorites
157 
158 On the Music interface, click ![collect](../common/collect.svg) to add music to My Favorites.
159 
160 The prompt information **Successfully added to "My favorites"** pops up and the music is added to **My Favorites** list.
161 
162 > ![notes](../common/notes.svg) Notes: You can also right-click the music, and select **Add to playlist** > **My favorites** to add the music to **My Favorites** list.
163 
164 ### View Lyrics
165 
166 On the Music interface, you can:
167 
168 - Click ![lyric_normal](../common/lyric_normal.svg) to switch to the lyrics interface. Click it again to fold the lyrics interface.
169 
170 - Click ![music](../common/music.svg)to switch to the lyrics interface. Click it again to fold the lyrics interface.
171 
172 
173 > ![notes](../common/notes.svg) Notes: If the music being played has a cover picture, ![music](../common/music.svg)will be displayed as the cover picture. Click it to switch to the lyrics interface and click it again to fold the lyrics interface.
174 
175 ### Adjust Play Mode
176 
177 On the Music interface, click ![single](../common/single_tune_circulation_normal.svg) , ![cross_cycling_normal](../common/cross_cycling_normal.svg) or ![sequential_loop_normal](../common/sequential_loop_normal.svg) to adjust the play mode.
178 
179 ![single](../common/single_tune_circulation_normal.svg): Single loop
180 
181 ![cross_cycling_normal](../common/cross_cycling_normal.svg): Shuffle
182 
183 ![sequential_loop_normal](../common/sequential_loop_normal.svg): List loop

193 
194 On the Music interface, right-click the music and you can:
195 
196 - Click **Remove from playlist** to remove the music from the playlist, but the music file will still be saved on the local disk.
197 - Click **Delete from local disk** to remove the music both from the playlist and the local disk.
198 
199 ![0|remove](fig/remove.png)

202 
203 1. On the Music interface, right-click the music.
204 2. Click **Encoding**, and you can select the encoding as needed.

206 ![0|encoding](fig/encoding.png)

209 
210 1. On the Music interface, right-click the music file in the playlist.
211 2. Click **Song info** to view the music title, artist, album, type, size, duration, path, and other information.

215 ## Playlist Management
216 
217 On the Music interface, you can create, delete, and rename playlist. You can also add music to the playlist.
218 
219 > ![notes](../common/notes.svg) Notes: **All music** and **My Favorites **are set as default playlists and cannot be deleted. Custom playlists are subsets of the **All Music **playlist. **My Favorites **playlist can be managed directly or through the Favorite button on the main interface.
220 
221 ### Create New Playlist
222 
223 1. On the Music interface, click ![icon_plus](../common/icon_plus.svg) right to the **Playlists**, or click ![menu](../common/icon_menu.svg)> **Add playlist**.
224 2. Enter the name of the playlist.
225 3. Press the **Enter** key on keyboard.
226 
227 ![0|addlist](fig/addlist.png)
228 
229 ### Rename Playlist
230 
231 1. Select a playlist from **Playlists**.
232 2. Right-click and select **Rename**.
233 3. Enter the name of the playlist, and press the **Enter** key on keyboard to save it.
234 
235 ![0|renamelist](fig/renamelist.png)
236 
237 ### Add Music to Playlist
238 
239 1. On the Music interface, select the music you want to add.
240 2. Right-click and select **Add to playlist** to add the music to the selected playlist.
241 
242 > ![tips](../common/tips.svg)Tips: You can drag the local music to a specified playlist to add the music into the current playlist and **All Music** playlist.
243 
244 ![0|addtolist](fig/addtolist.png)
245 
246 ### Remove Music from Playlist
247 
248 1. Select a playlist from **Playlists**, and select the music you want to remove from it.
249 2. Right-click and select **Remove from playlist** to remove the music from the playlist.
250 
251 ![0|remove](fig/removefromlist.png)
252 
253 ### Delete Playlist
254 
255 1. Select a playlist from **Playlists**.
256 2. Right-click the playlist to be deleted, and select **Delete**.
257 3. In the pop-up window, select **Delete** to confirm the operation.

261 ## Main Menu
262 
263 In the main menu, you can [Add Music](#Add Music), set the equalizer and the options, switch window themes, view help manual, and get more information about Music.
264 
265 ### Equalizer
266 
267 You can set your favorite music mode, such as classical, rock, and so on.
268 
269 1. On the Music interface, click ![menu](../common/icon_menu.svg).
270 2. Select **Equalizer**.
271 3. You can switch on equalizer, customize **Preamplifier** values and frequencies, and click **Save**; or select a music mode from the drop-down list which will be automatically saved by the system.
272 4. Click **Restore Defaults** to restore the default settings.
273 
274 ![equalizer](fig/equalizer.png)
275 
276 ### Settings
277 
278 You can set the basic information and shortcuts in settings.
279 
280 #### Basic Settings
281 
282 1. On the Music interface, click ![menu](../common/icon_menu.svg).
283 2. Select **Settings** > **Basic**.
284  - In the option **Play**, you can:
285  - Check the **Autoplay** box, and the music will be played automatically when Music runs.
286  - Check the **Remember playback position** box, and the music will be played automatically from the last position played when Music runs.
287  - Check the **Enable fade in/out** box, and the music will fade in and out when Music runs.
288  - In the option **Close Main Window**, you can:
289  - Select **Minimize to system tray** to close the main window, and hide Music to system tray.
290  - Select **Exit** to close the main window, and exit Music.
291  - Select **Ask me always** to prompt system notification, and confirm whether to exit Music when the main window is closed.
292 3. Click **Restore Defaults** to restore the default settings.
293 
294 #### Shortcuts
295 
296 1. On the Music interface, click ![menu](../common/icon_menu.svg).
297 2. Select **Settings** > **Shortcuts** to view the shortcuts.
298 3. You can select and re-enter shortcut keys to customize your choice.
